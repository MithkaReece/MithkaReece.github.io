DurJam Re-Sources

DevPost

Development - August 2020

During a brief four-day event called DurJam, our team of four embarked on creating a distinctive survival game. This endeavor marked our first experience collaborating on a project, underscoring the significance of version control. Initially, we employed an online editor, Glitch, where we all simultaneously contributed code. While this enabled us to observe each other's code as it was being written, a notable challenge emerged: the inability to test incomplete code. This limitation was less pronounced given our short timeline, but it prompted us to transition to using GitHub. This allowed us to work concurrently, merging our efforts at the project's conclusion.

During this venture, we achieved several milestones. These encompassed the creation of procedurally generated planets, implementation of rotating gravity at planet corners—permitting players to traverse the entire planet—resource collection mechanics, an inventory system, and functional survival indicators like oxygen and food levels. Players could then embark on interplanetary journeys by boarding a ship (activated with the 'f' key) and flying to new planets.

However, certain objectives remained unfinished. These included incorporating specific ores on planets, introducing greater variety to planet environments, developing a crafting system, and implementing the mechanics for loading resources to fuel the rocket.

This endeavor not only resulted in a functional survival game prototype but also offered a firsthand lesson on the importance of version control and collaborative development.
