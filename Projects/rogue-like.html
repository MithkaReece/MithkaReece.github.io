Previous Development - Summer 2022

Initially conceived as a group project, our aim was to create a basic Rogue-Like Game and collectively acquire game development skills. However, due to various commitments, most members were unable to contribute significantly. A few members provided minimal input, such as a handful of concept art pieces and a rudimentary framework for a stats system.

Given the limited contributions and lack of ongoing commitment from other team members, coupled with the fact that the majority of the progress up to that point was driven by my efforts, I made the decision to transform this endeavor into a solo project. This choice affords me the opportunity to further refine my game development abilities.

Current Development - Summer 2023

My current objective is to finalize the fundamental systems within the game, including combat mechanics, procedurally generated levels and a basic progression system. This will complete the core gameplay loop. Once these components are in place, I intend to consider the game in a finished state, yet retaining the flexibility to introduce new content as required.

Subsequently, refining the game to a polished state will allow me to incorporate additional elements such as particle effects, visual enhancements, optimization strategies and ensuring the game's compatibility across a range of devices capable of running it.

Gameplay

This video showcases the primary focus of the project, the combat system. The mechanics employ state machines to govern both player and enemy actions along with their corresponding animations. Communication between the player and enemy is achieved through events, influencing their states.

The combat system draws inspiration from games like Sekiro and Souls-Like titles. Players can employ actions such as rolling to evade attacks, blocking to mitigate damage, and executing well-timed parries to stun enemies. A distinctive feature is the poise meter, depicted as a purple circle that fills up. When fully charged, it results in stunning the opponent and inflicting substantial damage. This effect, however, diminishes shortly after, making it possible only after a sequence of consecutive attacks. The circular indicators below the player and enemy portraits illustrate their remaining health.

Notably, the weapon sprites are dynamically positioned and animated through transformations. This dynamic framework permits the seamless swapping of weapon sprites, thereby enabling multiple weapons to share specific attack animations, with the animations being character-dependent. Both the player and enemy share the same stick character base for their animations, differentiating only by the addition of clothing overlays for the player. This smart approach allows animations to be shared between players and enemies.

Additionally, I explored the conversion of 3D animations into 2D pixel art animations. This technique simplifies character manipulation and creation, eliminating the need to redraw animations. Dead Cells uses a similar method to streamline the animation process.
