Development - July-Current 2023

To enhance my proficiency in C++ and grasp the concept of ECS (Entity Component System), I embarked on re-creating the classic Asteroids game. For the visual elements, I employed the SFML library. This endeavor involved the development of a mechanism to manipulate and navigate through various game scenes, accomplished through the implementation of a scene manager.

The scene manager functions by overseeing distinct scenes, each responsible for monitoring entities and systems. These scenes can be generated, removed, or paused, consequently affecting the entities and systems within them. Complementing this, a separate system manager oversees the creation, deletion, and updates of the active systems.

The coordination between systems, the scene manager, and the system manager is achieved through event communication facilitated by the entt event dispatcher. The rest of the Asteroid game is constructed based on this fresh structure, maintaining the gameplay reminiscent of my earlier JS version. In addition to the core gameplay, I introduced new elements such as a main menu, a pause menu, and the functionality to save high scores.

As a next step, my intention is to refine the game further by incorporating particle effects, sound effects, optimization measures, and visual enhancements.
