Development - September 2020

I embarked on a modest reimagining of the Age of Empires DS game, driven by my desire to explore the creation of a turn-based game with elements like resource gathering and a non-orthographic 2D perspective. My progress in this endeavor involved several key achievements.

I successfully integrated terrain generation, enabling the creation of diverse landscapes. Resource collection mechanics were implemented, allowing players to gather essential resources. Building menus and interactions were developed, providing players with the means to construct structures and engage with them.

Additionally, I tackled the intricacies of training and maneuvering friendly units. One noteworthy aspect was the turn-changing mechanism, enabling alternating player movements. To facilitate player movement, an algorithm was devised to calculate feasible unit paths based on movement capabilities and terrain influences. These valid paths were highlighted and could be selected by players for unit movement.

One significant task left to implement was the combat system itself. Although not overly complex, it involved interactions between opposing unit statistics. A more intricate system lay in the realm of buffs, where enemies were granted advantages contingent on the terrain type they occupied.

This project allowed me to grasp the foundations of turn-based gameplay, resource management and unit interactions, while also offering insights into the complexities of terrain-based effects on gameplay mechanics.

NOTE

To begin with, it's important to mention that due to the constraints posed by limited sprites, there's a divergence between the visual representation and the actual in-game elements. Although the sprite may depict two villagers, players actually commence with just one villager, a unit capable of constructing structures. Additionally, each player starts with a militia unit, a foundational combat unit (although the combat functionality hasn't been implemented yet). Furthermore, buildings that lack their own dedicated sprites are temporarily denoted by the blue townhall sprite, serving as a stand-in for the time being.

Gameplay

The demo showcases the gameplay mechanics of Age of Empires: Age of Kings, a turn-based strategy game. In this game, resources such as food and gold are integral and each unit is permitted specific actions per turn. When units are selected, their movement capabilities are visually highlighted. Once a unit is moved to a location, a user interface (UI) offers a range of potential actions possible for that square. Upon pressing "Enter," the turn shifts to the next player, allowing them to take their actions. It's worth noting that players can only manipulate their own units and interact with their own buildings.

The terrain is procedurally generated, incorporating diverse terrain square types. These squares impact the distance units can travel across them. Additionally, gold and food deposits are strategically placed, which villagers can utilize for construction. Certain buildings, highlighted in blue, can serve as foundations for the construction of other buildings. The game features several building types, including mills, mines, townhalls, castles, stables, and barracks. Mills and mines yield food or gold every turn, while townhalls can train villagers in one turn using up these resources. The intention was to expand this functionality to stables and barracks for training combat units.

The sidebar UI incorporates various options, such as settings, main menu, map view and an "unmoved unit finder" feature. A notable enhancement in this version compared to the DS version is the integration of mouse control, which offers a more efficient gameplay experience. However, traditional controls are retained, allowing players to traverse squares using WASD and select them using the space key.
